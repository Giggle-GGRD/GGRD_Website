FAQ ❓

Quick answers to common questions.

What is GGRD?
GGRD (Giggle Reloaded) is a fixed-supply meme token on BNB Smart Chain with 10,000,000 tokens. It combines meme culture with real on-chain mechanisms: charity vesting, staking, and multisig governance. It is not a security or investment product.
How does the presale work?
The presale has 30 stages with prices from $0.005 to $1.20 (240x growth). Stages 1–25 are sell-out based — no time limit, each stage ends when its token pool is sold. Stages 26–30 have fixed deadlines with unsold tokens rolling to the next stage. After stage 30, any remaining tokens are burned permanently. Target raise: ~$1.4M USDC, with 80% going to the liquidity pool.
How does the charity mechanism work?
2,000,000 GGRD (20% of supply) is locked in the CharityVesting403030 contract with a 1-year cliff and 3-year linear vesting. Funds are split 40/30/30 across three beneficiaries selected through community governance. No tokens can be claimed before March 2027. Read the full Donation Policy.
How does staking work?
10% APR with a 30-day minimum lock period. The reward pool is capped at 3,000,000 GGRD (hardcoded on-chain). Maximum total staked: 5,000,000 GGRD. Early exit incurs a 10% penalty. Program duration: 730 days.
Who controls the funds?
All treasury wallets (governance, liquidity, marketing) are controlled by 2-of-3 Safe multisig wallets. Critical operations require 48h or 72h timelocks. No single person can move funds unilaterally. All multisig addresses are listed in the Contracts section.
Is there a transfer tax?
0% tax. There is no fee mechanism in the token contract. No buy tax, no sell tax, no transfer tax. The contract does not contain any fee-related functions.
Can more tokens be minted?
No. The total supply of 10,000,000 GGRD was minted once in the constructor. The contract contains no mint function. Supply is fixed forever.
Where can I verify the contracts?
All contracts are verified on BscScan — see the Contract Addresses section below. Full source code is also available on GitHub. The Whitepaper v4.0 documents every parameter with contract-level references.
