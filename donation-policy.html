This policy outlines how the GGRD Charity Treasury (10% of total supply) is managed and distributed to support pediatric oncology care.

1. Charitable Purpose

The GGRD Charity Treasury holds 100,000 GGRD (10% of total supply) in a dedicated Squads 3/4 multisig wallet. Its exclusive purpose is to fund on-chain crypto donations for:

  • Pediatric oncology support ‚Äî children with cancer and their families
  • Medical treatment funding ‚Äî chemotherapy, radiation, surgeries, medications
  • Family support services ‚Äî accommodation, transportation, psychological counseling
  • Medical research ‚Äî pediatric cancer research initiatives (secondary priority)

2. Beneficiary Selection Process

2.1. Eligibility Criteria

To qualify as a beneficiary, charitable organizations must meet ALL of the following requirements:

‚úÖ Mandatory Requirements

  1. Registration & Legal Status
    • Registered non-profit or charity in their jurisdiction
    • Valid tax-exempt status (501(c)(3) in US, or equivalent)
    • At least 2 years of operational history
  2. Financial Transparency
    • Public financial reports (annual revenue, expenses, allocation)
    • Third-party audit or review (e.g., GuideStar, Charity Navigator rating)
    • Administrative overhead below 25% of total budget
  3. Mission Alignment
    • Primary focus on pediatric cancer or pediatric oncology
    • Direct service delivery (not just fundraising intermediaries)
    • Measurable impact metrics published regularly
  4. Cryptocurrency Acceptance
    • Ability to receive crypto donations (native or via payment processor)
    • Published crypto wallet address or payment gateway link
    • Transparent conversion and usage policy for crypto donations
  5. No Conflicts of Interest
    • No financial ties to GGRD team members, multisig signers, or major holders
    • No political or religious affiliation that conflicts with project values
    • No history of fraud, mismanagement, or legal violations

2.2. Selection Workflow

Beneficiaries are selected through a community-driven governance process:

üìã Step-by-Step Process

  1. Nomination Phase (Days 1-7)
    • Community members submit nominations via Telegram or governance forum
    • Nominations must include: organization name, website, mission statement, proof of eligibility
    • GGRD core team performs initial verification of eligibility criteria
  2. Verification Phase (Days 8-14)
    • Core team conducts due diligence: legal status, financial reports, crypto capability
    • Organizations with missing criteria are removed from consideration
    • Verified candidates published in a public shortlist
  3. Community Discussion (Days 15-21)
    • Open forum discussion about shortlisted organizations
    • Community members share insights, concerns, preferences
    • Organizations may be contacted for additional information
  4. Voting Phase (Days 22-28)
    • On-chain governance vote using GGRD token (or wrapped/staked GGRD)
    • Voting weight: 1 GGRD = 1 vote (or quadratic voting to reduce whale influence)
    • Minimum quorum: 5% of circulating supply must participate
  5. Execution Phase (Days 29-35)
    • Top-voted organization(s) selected as beneficiary
    • Multisig signers approve donation transaction
    • On-chain transaction executed with public transaction hash
    • Announcement and transparency report published

2.3. Voting Mechanics

  • Voting Power: Based on GGRD holdings (snapshot taken at start of voting period)
  • Minimum Holding: Users must hold at least 100 GGRD to vote (prevents sybil attacks)
  • Vote Options: Each holder can vote for ONE organization from the shortlist
  • Transparency: All votes are on-chain and publicly verifiable

3. Donation Schedule & Mechanics

3.1. Frequency

Donations are executed approximately monthly or quarterly depending on:

  • Market conditions and GGRD token liquidity
  • Charity Treasury balance and conversion impact
  • Community governance decisions

3.2. Donation Amounts

Each donation batch follows these guidelines:

  • Target: ~10,000 GGRD per month (10-month total distribution schedule)
  • Flexibility: Amount may vary based on market cap and governance votes
  • Maximum Single Donation: No more than 20,000 GGRD at once (to limit market impact)

3.3. Conversion Process

GGRD tokens are converted to stable assets (USDC) using the following safeguards:

üõ°Ô∏è Market Impact Protection

  • TWAP/DCA Strategy: Swaps spread over time using Time-Weighted Average Price
  • Volume Limits: Maximum 5% of daily trading volume per swap
  • Slippage Limits: Maximum 2% slippage per transaction
  • Price Monitoring: Automated alerts if price impact exceeds thresholds
  • Pause Mechanism: Multisig can temporarily suspend conversions during extreme volatility

  • ‚ùå Liquidity provision or market-making
  • ‚ùå Exchange listing fees or paid promotions

5. Transparency & Reporting

5.1. Public Dashboards

The following information is publicly accessible:

  • Real-time Treasury Balance: Solscan/Explorer link to multisig wallet
  • Transaction History: All incoming/outgoing transactions with timestamps
  • Donation Log: Complete list of beneficiaries, amounts, dates, tx hashes

5.2. Monthly Reports

After each donation, a public report is published containing:

  • Beneficiary organization name and verification details
  • GGRD amount donated and USD equivalent at time of donation
  • On-chain transaction hash for full verification
  • Market impact analysis (price before/after, volume)
  • Remaining Charity Treasury balance

5.3. Annual Summary

At the end of each year, a comprehensive report includes:

  • Total GGRD donated and total USD equivalent
  • List of all beneficiary organizations supported
  • Impact metrics (children helped, treatments funded, etc.)
  • Community governance participation statistics
